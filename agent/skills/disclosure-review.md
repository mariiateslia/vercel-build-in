# Disclosure Review Playbook

## Step 1 — Company profile
From the notes, infer: public vs private, industry, financing (debt/leases),
revenue model, related parties, customer/supplier concentration, tax posture.
Summarize in one or two sentences plus a short attribute list.

## Step 2 — Map applicable ASC topics
Match what the notes describe to topics. Common set:
- ASC 606 Revenue Recognition · ASC 842 Leases · ASC 740 Income Taxes
- ASC 850 Related Parties · ASC 855 Subsequent Events · ASC 450 Contingencies
- ASC 820 Fair Value (if debt/fair-value relevant)
- Concentration-risk disclosures (if major customers/suppliers exist)
Give a one-line rationale per topic tied to the notes.

## Step 3 — Check for disclosure gaps
For each applicable topic, test whether required disclosures are present, weak,
or unclear. High-yield checks:
- Leases: variable lease payments (CAM/CPI/usage), maturity schedule, discount rate, ROU asset detail.
- Revenue/AR: concentration of credit risk (single customer % of revenue or AR).
- Debt: nature of covenants, compliance, waivers/amendments, unused facilities.
- Related parties: terms, amounts, duration of arrangements.
- Contingencies / commitments not reflected in the notes.
- Significant estimates not described.
- Subsequent events beyond the boilerplate evaluation sentence.
Assign each finding a status and a confidence, explain why it matters in context,
and attach one management question.

## Step 4 — Management questions
Turn the gaps into clear, answerable questions for management.

## Step 5 — Client email
Draft a short, professional email: brief intro, numbered list of the few most
important asks (pull from the findings), and a closing line.
